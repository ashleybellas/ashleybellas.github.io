Hello and Welcome!


Thank you for visiting my homepage. Here you'll find the research I published as partial fulfillment of my degree, PhD in Geophysics. I specialize in computational geodynamics with particular interest in lithospheric dynamics, rheology, and flexure. I am now a postdoctoral fellow at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where I am exploring analytic techniques relevant to the dynamics and structure of Earth's lower mantle.

The fundamental questions that drive my research include: what is the nature of the lithospheric rheology that gives rise to the unique tectonic style of Earth? Are the methods and models used to constrain rheology consistent with each other and with field observations of real Earth systems? What are the basic physical principles that govern the dynamic and structural evolution of terrestrial bodies?

I am highly interested in making connections with scientists of similar and dissimilar expertise, so I encourage you to reach out if you see potential for us to collaborate!
